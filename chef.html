Chef & Nutrition

Supplements that work. Meals that taste good. All built around your goals.

Performance
Creatine Monohydrate
The single most researched supplement in sports science. Works for nearly everyone.
Dose
3–5g daily
When
Any time — consistency matters
Replenishes ATP faster during high-intensity efforts. Increases strength, power, muscle volume, and even cognitive function. No loading phase needed — just take it every day.
Performance
Whey Protein
Fast-absorbing complete protein. The most convenient way to hit your daily protein target.
Dose
20–40g per serving
When
Post-workout or any time protein is low
Whey is a complete protein with a high leucine content — the amino acid that triggers muscle protein synthesis. Use it to fill protein gaps, not replace whole food meals.
Performance
Caffeine
The most proven performance-enhancing compound you can legally take.
Dose
3–6 mg/kg bodyweight
When
30–60 min pre-workout
Blocks adenosine receptors reducing perceived exertion, increases power output and endurance. Coffee works just as well as pre-workout. Cycle off periodically to maintain sensitivity.
Recovery
Magnesium Glycinate
Over 70% of people are deficient. Critical for muscle function, sleep, and recovery.
Dose
200–400mg elemental
When
30–60 min before bed
Glycinate form has the best absorption and is gentlest on the stomach. Improves sleep quality, reduces muscle cramps, supports 300+ enzymatic reactions. One of the most impactful supplements for athletes.
Recovery
Ashwagandha (KSM-66)
Adaptogen that lowers cortisol, improves strength, and enhances sleep quality.
Dose
600mg daily (KSM-66)
When
Morning or before bed
Clinical trials show KSM-66 ashwagandha significantly reduces cortisol and stress markers. Also shown to increase testosterone modestly and improve VO2 max. Takes 4–8 weeks for full effect.
Recovery
Electrolytes
Sodium, potassium, and magnesium. Essential if you sweat heavily or train in heat.
Dose
1 serving intra or post-workout
When
During or after intense training
Electrolyte imbalance is a major cause of cramping, fatigue, and poor performance. Avoid sugar-loaded sports drinks — choose low/no sugar options with meaningful sodium content (500mg+).
General Health
Fish Oil (Omega-3)
EPA and DHA reduce inflammation, support joint health, heart health, and brain function.
Dose
2–3g EPA+DHA daily
When
With a meal containing fat
Most people have an extremely high omega-6 to omega-3 ratio from modern diets — fish oil corrects this. Look at the EPA+DHA content on the label, not just total fish oil. Take with food to avoid fishy burps.
General Health
Vitamin D3 + K2
Critical for immune function, testosterone production, bone health, and mood.
Dose
2,000–5,000 IU D3 + 100mcg K2
When
Morning with a fat-containing meal
Most people in northern climates are deficient year-round. K2 (MK-7 form) ensures calcium goes to bones, not arteries — always take with D3. Get bloodwork done to dial in your dose.
General Health
Zinc
Supports testosterone production, immune health, and wound healing. Depleted by hard training.
Dose
15–30mg elemental zinc
When
Before bed, away from calcium
Athletes lose zinc through sweat. Deficiency directly suppresses testosterone and immune function. Don't take on an empty stomach — it can cause nausea. Picolinate and bisglycinate forms absorb best.
Recovery
Collagen Peptides
Supports joint health, tendons, ligaments, and skin. Underrated for long-term training longevity.
Dose
10–15g daily
When
Before training with vitamin C
Collagen synthesis peaks 30–60 min after ingestion — taking it before exercise maximizes delivery to tendons and joints. Combine with 50mg vitamin C for significantly better absorption. Great for injury prevention.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole egg
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • 2 tbsp feta cheese
  • ¼ cup diced onion
  • Cooking spray
  • Salt, pepper, red pepper flakes
  • 1 slice whole grain toast
Instructions
  1. 1Whisk egg whites and whole egg together with a pinch of salt and pepper. Set aside.
  2. 2Spray a non-stick pan with cooking spray. Over medium heat, sauté diced onion for 2 min until softened. Add spinach and cook until wilted, about 1 min.

  • ½ cup carrots, sliced
  • 3 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tsp fresh ginger, minced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp cornstarch + 2 tbsp water
